Cozumel Historical Information and Tours

Cozumel has a rich and diverse history. It is believed that the Maya first settled on Cozumel in the early part of the 1st millennium AD. There are a number of Mayan ruins on the island, and although many have been destroyed, the largest remaining ruin can be found at San Gervasio, located near the center of the island. Mayan Ruins

The island of Cozumel, as Mexico’s largest Caribbean island, is about 30 miles long and 10 miles wide. The majority of the island’s population lives in San Miguel located on Cozumel’s western shore.

Large parts of the island are covered with mangrove forest and endemic animal species, two of which are the Pygmy Raccoon and the Dwarf Coati Nasua. These two species are “critically endangered”.
Pygmy Raccoon | Cozumel Coati

Among the other endemic fauna are:

  • Cozumel Emerald Hummingbird
  • Splendid Toadfish
  • Black Iguana
  • Cozumel Fox

Cozumel is almost entirely surrounded by splendid coral reefs, making scuba diving and snorkeling two of the main attractions of the island. Snorkeling Tours
